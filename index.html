The JCI Regatta Story

JCI Regatta had its humble beginnings at Santa Maria Parish Church where its founders have met path to establish the local organization. 2009 National President Fulbert Woo, 2009 JCI Iloilo President Rex Chua, and JCI Regatta Charter President Elmer Usi invited the members of the Santa Maria Parish and their network of young individuals to join JCI Regatta.

What is the Junior Chamber International Regatta?

JCI-Regatta, established in Iloilo in 2009, derives its name from the Paraw Regatta Festival and strives to promote Iloilo, the festival, and cultivate leaders for positive community impact.

What are the activities involved in the Junior Chamber International Regatta?

JCI-Regatta empowers members through diverse opportunities in Business & Entrepreneurship, International Cooperation, Individual Development, and Community Impact, engaging in leadership development, local and international conferences, fundraisers, community projects, and conventions and competitions, fostering personal growth and societal awareness.

Who can participate in the Junior Chamber International Regatta?

JCI-Regatta welcomes Ilonggos aged 18 to 40, driven by vision and compassion, believing in their capacity to create impactful community change.

How do I become a JCI Regatta member?

To become a JCI Regatta member, message our page at JCI Regatta and participate in an Opportunity to Impact orientation.

How can I get involved in the Junior Chamber International Regatta?

To get involved with the organization, follow these five simple steps:

  • Fill out the membership form and create an account in www.jci.cc and www.jciphilippines.com
  • Attend in-house JCI trainings to know more about JCI
  • JCI Discover and JCI Explore
  • Attend projects and meetings
  • Pay Membership Dues
